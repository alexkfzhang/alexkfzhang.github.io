Research

My research interests lie in the general area of machine learning, optimization and computer vision. I am currently focused on building safe, robust and highly efficient deep (inverse) reinforcement learning algorithms with theoretical guarantees. I am also interested in developing algorithms for data driven self-driving systems with deep learning and reinforcement learning.

Publications

On Mode Collapse in Generative Adversarial Networks
Kaifeng Zhang
International Conference on Artificial Neural Networks (ICANN), 2021 (Oral)
GANs with supervision signal (SSGAN) introduces a supervision signal to alleviate the mode collapse issue in training GANs. The supervision signal tells the generator an approximate output corresponding to the input noise, which ensures the generated manifold to be close to the data manifold. Therefore, the generator could be able to better capture the whole data distribution.

Patents

A Generative Adversarial Nets based Intelligent Self Calibration Method
Kaifeng Zhang
PCT/CN2021/093935
This patent introduce a novel generative adversarial nets based approach to clibrate the steering systems of the cars (EPS). We combine trajectory matching, GANs, and a pre-trained neural network to learn the parameters of the EPS system with the aim of making the steering feeling similar to some calibrated cars.
